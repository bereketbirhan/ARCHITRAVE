Profile N°05

Le Corbusier

Switzerland · France — 1887–1965

Architect · Urban Planner · Theorist

01 / Biography

Life & Work

Charles-Édouard Jeanneret — known by his pseudonym Le Corbusier — was born in 1887 in La Chaux-de-Fonds, Switzerland, and became one of the most influential figures in the history of modern architecture.

Moving to Paris in 1917, he developed the "Five Points of Architecture" (pilotis, free plan, free façade, ribbon windows, and roof gardens) and the Modulor, a proportional system based on the human body, both of which shaped modernist design worldwide.

Beyond individual buildings, Le Corbusier was also a pioneering urban planner, most notably designing the master plan for Chandigarh, India — one of the few cities built largely from a single architect's vision.

03 / Portfolio

Famous Projects

Villa Savoye

Poissy, France — the definitive built statement of the Five Points, raised on slender pilotis.

Notre-Dame du Haut

Ronchamp, France — a sculptural concrete chapel that broke from his earlier machine-age purism.

Unité d'Habitation

Marseille, France — a self-contained vertical housing block that influenced postwar social housing worldwide.

04 / Ethos

Design Philosophy

Le Corbusier saw the house as a precise, functional instrument — "a machine for living in" — yet his later concrete work, like the chapel at Ronchamp, revealed an equally strong interest in sculptural, almost sacred form.

“A house is a machine for living in.”

— Le Corbusier
