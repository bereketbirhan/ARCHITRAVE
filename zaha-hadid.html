Profile N°01

Zaha Hadid

Iraq · United Kingdom — b. 1950

Architect · Designer · Pritzker Prize Laureate

01 / Biography

Life & Work

Dame Zaha Hadid (1950–2016) was one of the most influential architects of the twenty-first century. Born in Baghdad, Iraq, she later became a British citizen and transformed contemporary architecture through bold, futuristic designs.

She studied mathematics at the American University of Beirut before attending the Architectural Association School of Architecture in London. After founding Zaha Hadid Architects in 1980, she gained international recognition for buildings with flowing curves, dramatic forms, and innovative engineering.

Throughout her career, Hadid challenged conventional architectural thinking and inspired a new generation of designers. Her work can be found across Europe, Asia, the Middle East, and North America.

04 / Ethos

Design Philosophy

Hadid believed architecture should push beyond conventional boundaries. Her work embraced movement, fluidity, and innovation, creating spaces that felt dynamic rather than static, transforming how modern buildings are imagined and experienced.

“There are 360 degrees, so why stick to one?”

— Zaha Hadid
