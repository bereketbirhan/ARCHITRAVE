Profile N°04

Norman Foster

United Kingdom — b. 1935

Architect · Pritzker Prize Laureate

01 / Biography

Life & Work

Norman Foster (b. 1935) grew up in Stockport, near Manchester, England. He studied architecture and city planning at the University of Manchester before earning a Henry Fellowship to Yale University, where he met fellow architect Richard Rogers.

In 1967 he founded Foster Associates, later Foster + Partners, which grew into one of the world's largest architecture practices. Foster is widely regarded as a pioneer of high-tech architecture — buildings that expose their structure and services as part of the design.

His practice has become synonymous with energy-conscious, structurally expressive design, from airports and skyscrapers to bridges and master plans, working at a scale few architecture firms attempt.

04 / Ethos

Design Philosophy

Foster's work treats sustainability and structure as design drivers rather than afterthoughts — a building's skeleton, services, and energy performance are made legible rather than hidden. The result is architecture that reads as engineering made elegant.
